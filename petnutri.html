Pet Nutrition

Pet nutrition is essential for maintaining your pet's overall health, energy, and well-being. Just like humans, pets need a balanced diet that provides the right mix of nutrients — including proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals — to stay healthy.

Here are some tips:

  • Feed a Balanced Diet

    • Make sure your pet's food includes all the essential nutrients-proteins, fats, carbs, vitamins and minerals. Look for food labeled as "complete and balanced" for their life stage.

  • Watch the Portion Sizes

    • Overfeeding is one of the most common causes of pet obesity. Stick to the feeding guidelines on the package, and adjust based on your pet's activity level and weight.

  • Keep Water Fresh and Full

    • Just like us, pets need access to clean, fresh water at all times. Hydration helps with digestion, temperature, regulation and overall health.

  • Go Easy On Treats

    • Treats should make up no more than 10% of your pet's daily calories. And steer clear of giving pets human food like chocolate, grapes, onions etc can be toxic.

  • Feed According to Life Stage

    • Puppies and kittens need food with extra calories and nutrients,while senior pets may need lower-calorie or specialized diets.

  • Stick to a Routine

    • Feeding your pet at the same times each day helps with digestion and makes it easier to spot changes in appetite-which can be an early sign of illness.

  • Use Slow Feeders

    • These help prevent gulping, aid digestion and provide mental stimulation-great for fast eaters
